2FA Setup Guide

Step 1: Download & Install the App

Go to the App Store and search for "Authenticator: 2FA & Passkey". Click "Install" and open the app once installed.

Step 2: Find the QR Code or Manual Key

To enable 2FA, you’ll need a **QR code** or **manual key** from the service you’re securing. Here’s how to find it for most apps and services:

  • **Log in** to the account you want to secure (e.g., Google, Facebook, Dropbox).
  • Navigate to **Security Settings** or **Account Settings**.
  • Look for an option to enable **Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)** or **Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA)**.
  • Once enabled, you’ll typically see a QR code or a manual key. This is what you’ll scan or enter into the Authenticator app.

If you can’t find the option, look for help in the service’s **FAQ** or **Support** section by searching for "Two-Factor Authentication setup."

Step 3: Add Your Account to the Authenticator: 2FA & Passkey App

Open the "Authenticator: 2FA & Passkey" app and tap **"Add Account"**. Use the app’s camera to **scan the QR code** displayed on the service’s security page. If you don’t have a QR code, choose the option to manually enter the **key** provided by the service.

Step 4: Generate OTP Codes

Once the account is added, the app will generate **One-Time Passwords (OTPs)**. These codes refresh every 30 seconds and can be used to log into your service with enhanced security.

Step 5: Save Backup Codes

Many services offer backup codes during the 2FA setup. Save these codes in a safe location as a backup method to access your account if you lose your device.

Step 6: Test Your 2FA Setup

Go to the service where you enabled 2FA and log in. When prompted, open the "Authenticator: 2FA & Passkey" app and enter the code displayed. This ensures your account is securely protected with Two-Factor Authentication.
